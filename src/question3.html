Articles of incorporation

Articles of incorporation are a document submitted in order to formally register a business with a state. The document typically includes information such as the business’s name, members, and purpose. In some states, this document may be called a “certificate of formation.”

Corporation Bylaws

Corporation bylaws are a document stating the rules that govern the management and operation of a business. The document is typically signed by the company’s board of directors at the time of its founding.

Certificate of good standing

A certificate of good standing is a document stating that a corporation is approved to conduct business. You will need a certificate of good standing from each state in which the permitted activity would occur.

Resolution authorizing filing

A resolution authorizing filing is a formal document, signed by the corporation’s board of directors, stating that specific corporate officers are allowed to sign contracts on behalf of a corporation.
